# Catalyst Taxonomy

A *catalyst* is a dated, external reason to contact a studio now rather than later. Fit (the score) says whether they're worth pursuing; the catalyst says whether this week is the week. Sonar reads these from the research pass.

Two kinds — they act differently in `../rules.md`:

## Project catalysts — transactional timing
A live one lifts a Tier-2 fit (50–69) to **PURSUE** (rule 6). They signal that budget and audio scope are in motion now.
- **Announced ship date / launch window** — audio scope is locking now.
- **Trailer or gameplay reveal** — content polish; audio gaps are visible.
- **Funding round closed** — budget exists and is fresh. (Also clears the no-funding filter.)
- **Publishing deal signed** — external milestones now apply.
- **Audio job post** — they feel the gap; outsourcing is on the table. (Implies a budget exists.)
- **Studio expansion / new project announced** — new scope opening up.

## Relationship / excuse catalysts — a reason to reconnect
These don't require an open project. They are the **floor-extenders**: on an otherwise-DECLINE studio (e.g. unfunded), a live relationship catalyst lifts the verdict to **NURTURE** — keep the connection warm — never to PURSUE. See the Relationship floor in `../rules.md`.
- **GDC / GameSoundCon presence** — cheap face-time; a marathon, not a sprint.
- **A contact's job change** — a known face landed somewhere worth tracking.
- **A layoff / studio-trouble check-in** — a human reason to reach out, not a pitch.
- **A catch-up** — the relationship itself is overdue.
- **Contacts-CRM `Overdue = Yes`** — a built-in, dated catch-up excuse straight from the contact list (`contact-list.md`).

## Watch triggers (define the NURTURE entry)
For a NURTURE verdict, name which catalyst above Sonar should watch for. The entry is "ping when [trigger] fires" — often "ping on funding announcement" — not a vague "keep an eye on them."

## Non-catalysts (do not justify acting now)
- A studio simply existing and fitting the profile.
- Old news (older than ~6 months) with no fresh movement.
- "They make games we'd be good at" with no dated event.
